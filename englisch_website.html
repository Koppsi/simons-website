Virtual Reality Study with Unity

Bachelor's thesis on eHMIs

For my bachelor's thesis, I conducted a study on the topic of eHMIs (external Human-Machine-Interfaces). These are displays or lights mounted on the outside of vehicles that are intended to help communicate with various road users (e.g., pedestrians, cyclists, other manually driven car drivers) in the future with fully autonomous vehicles.

City in Unity

For this, I first used some assets from the Unity Asset Store to create a city. In this city, it should then be possible to test various scenarios with different road users (pedestrian, car driver, cyclist).

Simulators

To conduct a study, you have to navigate study participants through a scenario and for that, you need simulators. Here, VR glasses were used for each road user. With the pedestrian simulator, you can simply navigate through the simulation by walking. For the car driver, a steering wheel with pedals was added to drive the car in the virtual world. With the bicycle simulator, you have a real bicycle, a speedometer that measures the speed, and a wind machine that blows wind accordingly to simulate real wind.

Pedestrian

Here you can see the pedestrian's view. The pedestrian is supposed to cross the street and reach the green zone. The autonomously driving vehicle signals that it is letting the cyclist cross the street.

Manual Driver

Here you can see the driver's view. The driver is supposed to turn left and reach the green zone. There is a deadlock here because all cars want to turn left. The autonomously driving vehicle signals to the driver that it is giving him the right of way.

Cyclist

Here you can see the cyclist's view. The cyclist is supposed to go straight and reach the green zone. The autonomously driving vehicle signals that it has seen the cyclist.

Marvelous Mashup

Videospiel mit LibGDX

Marvelous Mashup is a small video game that was carried out in a group of 4-6 students in the 4th semester with LibGDX and Java. I was responsible for creating the user interfaces, screens, buttons, map, characters, and enemy AI. I also contributed to the editor. Here you can see the start screen of the video game.

Editor Screen

On the editor screen, you could choose between three different editors. You could adjust the game settings, the scenario settings, or the character settings.

Scenario Editor Screen

On the scenario editor screen, you can see the game field and can expand it with additional fields as desired. You can also change the type of field (portal, stone, grass) by clicking on fields.

Character Editor Screen

On the character editor screen, you can see all the characters that exist so far. By clicking on one of the characters, you can change and adjust the values of a character.

Team Selection Screen

The team selection screen appears after you have found another player to play with. You can now select 6 of the 12 characters to join your team.

Game Screen

After saving, you get to the game screen. The game begins and you have to get possession of all Infinity Stones to win the game. After a certain number of rounds, Thanos also appears to snatch all the stones himself.
